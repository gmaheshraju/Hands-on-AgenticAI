INTERNAL COMPLIANCE MEMORANDUM

TO: All Partners and Associates
FROM: Chief Compliance Officer
DATE: March 1, 2025
RE: Updated Client Data Handling Procedures — GDPR and CCPA Compliance

This memorandum outlines updated procedures for handling client data in accordance
with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the California Consumer
Privacy Act (CCPA).

1. SCOPE
These procedures apply to all attorneys, paralegals, and staff who access, process,
or store client personal data in any form, including electronic records, paper files,
and verbal communications.

2. DATA CLASSIFICATION
All client data must be classified into the following categories:
(a) Privileged Communications: Attorney-client privileged materials, including legal
opinions, strategy memoranda, and litigation hold notices;
(b) Sensitive Personal Data: Social Security numbers, financial account information,
health records, and biometric data — requires enhanced security controls;
(c) Standard Personal Data: Names, addresses, contact information, and employment
information — standard security controls apply;
(d) Public Information: Information that is publicly available through court filings,
regulatory databases, or public records.

3. DATA MINIMIZATION
(a) Collect only the minimum personal data necessary for the legal matter;
(b) Do not retain client data beyond the applicable retention period (see Section 6);
(c) Redact unnecessary personal identifiers in working documents;
(d) Use anonymization or pseudonymization where feasible for internal analysis.

4. CROSS-BORDER DATA TRANSFERS
(a) All transfers of personal data outside the European Economic Area (EEA) must be
subject to Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) or an approved transfer mechanism;
(b) Before transferring data, conduct a Transfer Impact Assessment (TIA) to evaluate
the legal framework of the recipient country;
(c) Maintain records of all cross-border transfers in the firm's data transfer register.

5. DATA SUBJECT ACCESS REQUESTS (DSAR)
(a) All DSARs must be forwarded to the Compliance Office within 24 hours of receipt;
(b) The firm must respond to DSARs within 30 days (GDPR) or 45 days (CCPA);
(c) Before disclosing any data, verify the identity of the requestor;
(d) Exemptions: The firm may withhold data protected by attorney-client privilege
or work product doctrine.

6. RETENTION AND DESTRUCTION
(a) Active Matter Files: Retained for the duration of the legal engagement;
(b) Closed Matter Files: Retained for seven (7) years from matter closure;
(c) Financial Records: Retained for ten (10) years per regulatory requirements;
(d) Destruction: Use certified shredding for physical documents and DOD 5220.22-M
compliant wiping for electronic media.

7. BREACH NOTIFICATION
(a) Report any suspected data breach to the Compliance Office immediately;
(b) The Compliance Office will assess the breach within 24 hours;
(c) GDPR breaches must be reported to the relevant supervisory authority within 72 hours;
(d) CCPA breaches requiring notification to affected individuals must be reported
without unreasonable delay.

8. TRAINING REQUIREMENTS
All personnel must complete the following training:
(a) Annual data protection training (mandatory);
(b) Specialized training for personnel handling sensitive personal data;
(c) Refresher training within 30 days of any significant regulatory update.

9. PENALTIES
Non-compliance with these procedures may result in:
(a) Disciplinary action, up to and including termination;
(b) Personal liability for violations of GDPR (fines up to 4% of global revenue)
or CCPA (statutory damages of $100-$750 per consumer per incident);
(c) Referral to the relevant bar association for ethical violations.

For questions, contact the Compliance Office at compliance@acmelegal.com.
